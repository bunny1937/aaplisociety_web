From your answers, the new billing engine needs to become config‑driven and extremely explicit about principal vs interest, while always following “interest satisfy first” as the default for payments.

Key ideas now locked in:

Monthly interest only, never forced daily. Society can configure:

INTEREST_ON_PRINCIPAL_ONLY (int = currInt on remaining principal), or

INTEREST_ON_PRINCIPAL_PLUS_PREV_INT (int = currInt + remInt).

Payment allocation rule:

Default: interest first (member’s total interest left).

Config: society chooses how “interest bucket” is defined:

INTEREST_USE_MODE = OLDEST_FIRST (clear oldest period’s interest first), or

INTEREST_USE_MODE = TOTAL (treat all interest as one pool, order doesn’t matter logically).

If member pays full bill before grace end date, no interest for that period (your Excel “0 + 1” pattern matches this).

Config-driven admin options:

payment breakdown visibility in member UI (transparent vs normal),

What the current system is doing
From your codebase and the Excel you shared, this is what the billing + interest system looks like right now:

Bills are per month per member, with:

previousBalance = sum of unpaid bill balances (from Bill), not from ledger.

interestAmount = calculated using calculateInterestAmount with:

anchor = getOldestDueDate(unpaidBills, billDueDay, billYear, billMonth)

grace period = society.config.gracePeriodDays

rate = society.config.interestRate

cap date = getBillPayFinalDate(...) so interest stops after a configured “final day”.

Interest logic is centralised in utils/interestUtils.js and reused in:

billing/excel-template (BillTemplate).

billing/generate (auto and from Excel).

bills/get-previous-balances.

lib/bill-renderer (for PDF/HTML bill).

payments/outstanding and ledger/calculate-interest for interest-on-arrears jobs.

Payment allocation is FIFO on bills:

In payments/record route, it fetches all Unpaid/Partial/Overdue bills ordered oldest first and allocates the new payment across their balanceAmount (principal+interest together).

After each allocation it recomputes bill status via calculateBillStatusAfterPayment(totalAmount, amountPaid) and updates Transaction with a Credit entry.

Excel example you shared models something different**:

Base maintenance = 1000 every month.

Interest at 21% p.a. as monthly 21/12≈1.75% on previous remaining only.

Unpaid base accumulates into “Previous Rem”, but note the line:

“Interest must be cleared first” → payments should go to interest component first, then principal.

Right now, your code does not honour "interest satisfy first": it pays against each bill’s balanceAmount without splitting “interest vs principal” per allocation step. Also the Excel has the notion of remaining interest (“remInt”) carrying forward and compounding on that, which the live code does not track explicitly per member/month.

Gaps between current system and your target
Based on that, these are the specific mismatches / places that need revamp:

No explicit split of principal vs interest per bill in allocation

Today: When paying, you reduce bill.balanceAmount and bill.amountPaid; no explicit fields for “interest cleared” vs “principal cleared”.

Needed for your rule: a deterministic rule like:

Clear oldest interest across all periods,

Then clear oldest principal.

The Excel logic suggests “previous remaining” and interest are tracked separately and interest is always serviced first.

Interest history vs monthly interest

Current system recalculates interest on total outstanding each new billing period using the anchor date and grace period, then stores the aggregate interestAmount for that period.

Excel example is more granular:

Interest in Feb: on 500 (Jan unpaid) → 9

March interest: 9 + 27, and some part “remInt” is carried to next month if not fully paid.

There is no “interest ledger” per member in your DB; it is derived from bills and from interest transactions in the ledger, but not in a form that can reconstruct “which rupee of interest is still unpaid”.

Payments vs interest cap date

There is a “payment window final date”: payment API blocks payment if today is beyond billPayFinalDay of oldest unpaid bill’s month.

Outstanding-interest calculation uses today’s month for cap, which is slightly different from “cap per-bill-month”.

For a robust “interest satisfy first” system, you probably want a single consistent rule:

interest accrues only until billPayFinalDate of each period, but payments are always allowed; it is only interest that stops accruing, not payments that stop.

Partial, over, late payments not fully modelled as per Excel

Partial (pay less than total) and overpayment (pay more than current outstanding) are handled in code, but only in terms of total balance, not in terms of the specific components they are clearing.

Late payments: separate cron ledger/calculate-interest adds extra “Interest” transactions day-wise in some societies, on top of bill-based interest; this can double count or conflict with the simple monthly interest logic you used in Excel.

Interest basis:

Config includes interestBasis = MONTHLY vs DAILY.

Your Excel is MONTHLY – Indian style (1 day late in a month → full month interest).

The code mostly aligns with that, but:

There is also DAILY mode for backward compatibility.

Your new rule may want to lock the society to only MONTHLY and disallow DAILY.

What the revamped design probably needs
Not changing code yet, just design-level understanding:

A clear, central rule for allocation (interest first):

For any payment:

Step 1: Determine total unpaid interest (from all unpaid bills and maybe outstanding-interest cron entries).

Step 2: Allocate payment to interest, starting from oldest interest component, until interest is zero or payment is exhausted.

Step 3: If payment remains, allocate to principal (bill charges) oldest-first.

This requires:

either explicit fields per bill: interestBalance, principalBalance,

or a reproducible way to reconstruct those from the ledger and bill history.

A unified interest model:

Decide one model:

A) Bill-period interest only (as in Excel: once per month, on previous remains).

B) Daily accrual via cron + some monthly bill representation.

Your Excel strongly suggests:

“Previous Rem” → principal,

Interest = (Previous Rem * 21% /12) plus any carried “remInt”.

That maps nicely to:

at bill generation: compute interest on outstanding, store interest for that period, and treat it as separate component.

Robust handling of scenarios:

No payment: interest keeps accruing until cap date.

Partial payment: first knocks off interest; if not enough, some interest remains; next month’s calculation uses remaining principal + remaining interest.

Overpayment: after clearing all interest and principal, either:

reject overpayment, or

create advance credit / negative balance and treat future bills as adjusted.

Late payment:

If payment arrives after due date, interest for that interval should already be captured (either in bill interest or by daily cron). Allocation rule still “interest first”.

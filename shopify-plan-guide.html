Additional Staff Accounts
The most misunderstood feature — and one of the biggest reasons to upgrade from Basic
🔑
Think of it like keys to your physical shop.
You own the master key (your Shopify owner login). But you can't be at the shop 24/7. So you make duplicate keys for your staff. Each duplicate key can be set to open only certain doors — the stockroom but not the safe, the counter but not the accounts. That's exactly what Shopify Staff Accounts do for your online store.
🤔 So what exactly IS a Staff Account?

A Staff Account is a separate login (email + password) that you create inside your Shopify admin for another person. That person can log in to your store's backend on their own device, at any time, and do their job — without ever knowing your password, and without being able to access things you haven't allowed.

Your Shopify store has ONE owner (you). The owner account is permanent and cannot be deleted or restricted. Staff accounts are additions on top of that — they're helpers, not co-owners.

🔐 Can you control what each staff can see or do?

Yes — completely. When you create a staff account, Shopify shows you a checklist of permissions. You tick what they're allowed to do. Nothing more, nothing less.

📦 Orders
View orders, process fulfillments, print packing slips, mark as shipped, issue refunds
🏷️ Products
Add new products, edit descriptions, update prices, manage inventory stock numbers
👤 Customers
View customer profiles, see order history, handle customer service queries
📊 Reports
View sales reports, traffic data, product performance — so your marketing team can make data decisions
🎨 Themes
Edit your store's design, update banners, change text — for your designer or content person
⚙️ Settings
Access to billing, payment settings, shipping — typically kept only for the owner

⚠️ Why NOT just share your main password with everyone?
🔓
No control. If someone has your main password, they can do anything — delete products, change prices, see all revenue, even change the owner email.
📝
No audit trail. With staff accounts, Shopify logs who did what and when. "Who deleted this product?" — you'll know.
🚪
Can't fire someone from the store. If a staff member leaves, just delete their account. With a shared password, you'd have to change it everywhere and risk them still having access.
🛡️
Security risk. Each staff account has its own 2FA. One compromised device doesn't expose your entire store owner account.

Your Business Policies
Written decisions about returns, refunds, shipping, and privacy
Important
WHY do I need this?

Every store legally needs policy pages — Refund Policy, Privacy Policy, Shipping Policy, and Terms of Service. These are not just legal protection for you — they're trust signals for your customers. When a customer wonders "what if I don't like it?" and finds a clear, fair refund policy, they buy. When they can't find one, they leave.

These policies also affect how Razorpay and payment gateways treat your account. "No stated policy" = higher dispute risk.

HOW to handle this — 3 options
A
You tell me your rules, I write the policies: Just answer these questions in a WhatsApp voice note or message — I'll draft professional policies from your answers. (Most common approach.)
B
You already have written policies: Send the text file or Google Doc. I'll clean it up and add it to the store.
C
Use Shopify's templates: Shopify has built-in policy templates. I'll use those as a base and customize with your specifics.
Decisions you need to make (just answer these):
• Do you accept returns? Within how many days? (7 days? 14 days? No returns?)
• Do you offer refunds or only store credit?
• Who pays return shipping — you or the customer?
• Are opened/used perfumes eligible for return? (Usually no.)
• What are your shipping timelines? (e.g., dispatch in 1–2 days, delivery in 3–7 days)
• Do you ship internationally? If yes, to which countries?
• Is COD (Cash on Delivery) available?
⚠️ If missing: Your store will go live without policy pages. It's not illegal — but it increases customer disputes and payment gateway scrutiny. Easy to add later, but better to do it right the first time.

Required
WHY do I need this?

This is done by updating two DNS records — think of it like updating the address in your GPS from "old location" to "Shopify's servers". Without this, customers who type your domain will see an error page instead of your store.

HOW to handle this
A
Tell me where you bought your domain. Common registrars: GoDaddy, Namecheap, BigRock, Google Domains, Hostinger. I'll tell you exactly where to click.

C
What I'll actually change: Only 2 things — the "A Record" (points to Shopify's IP) and a "CNAME" record for www. Nothing else. I won't touch your email settings or anything else.
D
Timing: DNS changes take 24–48 hours to propagate worldwide. Plan your launch date around this.
⚠️ If not shared: Your store will be live at a myshopify.com URL, not your own domain. Fine for testing — NOT for a real launch.
